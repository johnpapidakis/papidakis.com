As a student at Queen Mary, University of London, I am pursuing a Bachelor's degree in Electrical and Electronic Engineering and have a strong background in electrical engineering principles and programming languages such as Java, C, Python and SQL.

In my first year at Queen Mary, I secured an overall grade of 85.9% and I am currently on track for a first classification in my second year. Academically, I have done extensive coursework to make myself technically competent in microcontrollers , design of IC and coding (C, java, Python, Assembly Language) and I am eager to further implement these concepts in my work.  I also served as the Events Organizer and Vice President for the Artificial Intelligence and Robotic Society at Queen Mary. As a member of the committee, I have been actively involved in promoting the study and application of artificial intelligence and robotics among our members. Throughout the academic year, I have helped organize a range of events and activities, such as workshops, talks, and demonstrations on relevant topics, as well as competitions and projects that encourage members to apply their knowledge and skills. I have also helped invite guest speakers from academia and industry to share their expertise and experiences with our members. Additionally, I have been involved in collaborating with other organizations and universities, such as Imperial's College robotics club and King's College robotics society, to expand the reach and impact of our society.

In addition to my coursework, I have gained valuable experience through internships and extracurricular activities. For instance, ​during my internship at Robenso, I worked on a project to develop a reverse vending machine (RVM) that sorts recycling bottles based on their material. /there i had the chance to learn SQL and apply my knowledge to create a database that would store 1-Dimensional barcodes. oded a linear motor using serial communication between Python and Arduino, and installed a conveyor belt which I designed and 3D printed using SolidWorks. This project allowed me to gain experience in developing and implementing robotics solutions for waste management. 
Additionally, the past summer, I had the opportunity to work as an intern at Sultanas Export Union in an effort to optimize machine layouts to reduce unnecessary transit time during operation. Finally, I operated and performed adjustments on color sorting and X-Ray integration equipment. These experiences have given me practical skills in troubleshooting and operating complex machinery.

In conclusion, I am a highly motivated electrical engineer with a strong educational background and practical experience in the field. I am eager to continue expanding my knowledge and skills through new challenges and opportunities.
